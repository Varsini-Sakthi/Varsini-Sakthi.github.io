01 · Research focus

Single-cell and spatial genomics, read through a machine-learning lens.

My work sits at the intersection of computational biology and applied ML: building pipelines and models that turn single-cell and spatial transcriptomic data into interpretable biology. Most recently, that has meant characterizing microglial transcriptional programmes across brain development and benchmarking deep generative models against classical methods for cell representation.

I am interested in pursuing a PhD in computational biology, with a research interest in applying AI/ML methods to Alzheimer's disease and neurodegeneration, using single-cell and spatial data, alongside structure-based approaches to disease-relevant ligand interactions, to understand how cell states shift across disease progression.

Research questions I keep coming back to

  • How do cell states shift across neurodegenerative disease progression, and what does that shift look like at single-cell and spatial resolution?
  • When do deep generative models offer a real biological advantage over simpler baselines like PCA, and when does the added complexity not pay off?
  • Can attention-based architectures make spatial transcriptomics predictions interpretable enough to generate testable biological hypotheses, not just accurate ones?
